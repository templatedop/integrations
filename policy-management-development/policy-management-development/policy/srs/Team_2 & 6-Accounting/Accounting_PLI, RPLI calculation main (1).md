**[Introduction]{.underline}**

**[Postal Life Insurance]{.underline}**

Postal Life insurance was started in the year 1884. The Scheme initially
had a limited offer as a welfare measure to post and telegraph employees
and the extended to the employees of various departments of Central and
State Government.

**Types of PLI policies**

- Whole Life Assurance (Suraksha)

- Convertible Whole Life Assurance (Suvidha)

- Endowment Assurance (Santosh)

- Anticipated Endowment Assurance (Sumangal)

- Joint Life Assurance (Yugal Suraksha)

- Children's Policy (Bal JeevanBima)

**[RuralPostal Life Insurance]{.underline}**

The Rural Postal Life Insurance Scheme launched on 24.3.1995 to cover
Rural Public. The Scheme is operated as per the Notification issued by
the Govt. of India vide 'Rural Postal Life Insurance Schemes 1994.

**Types of RPLI policies**

- Whole Life Assurance (Gram Suraksha)

- Convertible Whole Life Assurance (Gram Suvidha)

- Endowment Assurance (Gram Santosh)

- Anticipated Endowment Assurance (Gram Sumangal)

- Ten Years RPLI (Gram Priya)

- Children's Policy (Bal JeevanBima)

**[Loan:-]{.underline}**

The loan may be repaid at any time. It may also be paid in installments
of amount not less than Rs. 100/-. Interest will be charged @ 10% per
annum compounding half yearly from the date of disbursement of loan and
should be paid on or before the dates specified in the loan bond and
loan repayment receipt book. Interest for the half year will be charged
on the amount outstanding on the first day of the half year, and any
repayment made during that half year will be taken into account for
calculation of interest only for the next half year. In the case of
final repayment, interest will not be charged beyond the last date of
the month in which the final repayment is made provided that interest
had already been charged on the loan for at least six months. The
responsibility for payment of interest rests solely on the insurant. A
notice regarding the amount to be paid as half yearly interest will be
issued to the insurant only when there is a change in the amount payable
as interest as a result of payment of a part of the principal.

But the plea of non-receipt of such a notice cannot in any circumstances
be accepted for non-payment of interest. If the interest is not paid on
the due date, it will be added to the outstanding amount of loan and
usual interest charged thereon. **(POLI Rule 59(3))**

**Loan Sanction Example:**

The following are the details of a Policy Number 1234 and Policyholder
A.

Product Name: Suraksha Issue Date : 25/11/2000

Policy Years: 12 years Surrender Value : 14,000 Rs.

Loan Application Date: 27/11/2012

So as of Loan Application date the policyholder A is eligible for 90% of
Surrender Value.

Maximum Loan Amount: 90% of Rs.14, 000 = Rs.12, 600

Minimum Loan Amount: Rs. 1000.

Thus a policyholder A will be eligible for Loan Amount between Minimum
Loan Amount and Maximum Loan Amount.

**Loan Closure**

The Process :- Loan Interest amount has to be paid as per the Repayment
Schedule given in Loan Bond.

• The loan interest payment has to be paid once every 6 months. In case
when Loan interest is not repaid within the due date the outstanding
loan interest is capitalized in case of non-payment of loan interest.

• On three consecutive defaults in Interest payment against loan, the
policy shall be surrendered.

• Currently any loan repayment is registered in the Loan repayment
receipt book. In case of ad-hoc repayments the Repayment schedule is
revised and interest on the Outstanding Loan amount is calculated in
next six monthly cycle.

• All the repayment details are updated in Loan Repayment receipt book.

**Loan Calculations**

• Policy loan is calculated as a percentage of surrender value.

• Maximum loan amount applicable shall be 90% of surrender value
provided the policy age has exceeded 12 years.

• The Loan interest rate is 10%, subject to change in future.

**[Revival of lapsed Policy:-]{.underline}*(Customer Portal of India
post)***

If the premia are not paid for 6 months in case of policy within 3
years, (or) 12 months in case of policy more than 3 years, then the
policy becomes lapsed. This needs revival to make it active. Revival may
be on any number of occasions during entire term of the policy, provided
a period of consecutive 5 (five) years should not have passed from the
date of first unpaid premium. After credit of due premium with interest
\@12% per annum and a good health certificate, the policy can be
revived. Revival amount may be paid in one lump sum or in convenient
installments not exceeding 12 installments.

Application for Revival - may be obtained from nearest Post Office/CPC
or may be downloaded from India Post website, PLI website or PLI
Customer Portal

Correct and complete filling up of Application for Revival supported
with Medical Certificate in prescribed performa.

Submission of duly filled-in Application for Revival, along with all
relevant documents, at any Post Office/CPC

Revival period and conditions:-

The rules for reviving a lapsed PLI policy are based on the duration of
non-payment from the first missed premium. 

Provide an employer certificate confirming no medical leave was taken
during the lapsed period (if applicable). 

- **Important note:** Policies that lapsed for a continuous period of
  five years from the date of the first unpaid premium became ineligible
  for revival after January 1, 2020, as per the Post Office Life
  Insurance Rules, 2011. 

**[Revival procedure:-]{.underline}**

1.  **Obtain the revival application form:** Contact the nearest Post
    Office or download the form from the [India Post
    website]{.underline}.

2.  **Fill out the application:** Complete the form with your personal
    and policy details, including the period and reason for non-payment.

3.  **Gather required documents:** This includes the application form,
    medical certificate, employer certificate, and your premium receipt
    book.

4.  **Pay arrears:** Calculate and pay the total outstanding premiums
    plus the 12% per annum interest. You can also inquire about the
    possibility of paying the revival amount in installments.

5.  **Submit documents:** Submit your application and documents to the
    Post Office where you intend to resume premium payments.

6.  **Medical examination (if required):** Depending on the duration of
    the lapse, a medical examination by an authorized doctor may be
    required to prove good health.

7.  **Department review:** The Department of Posts will review the
    application and medical reports before approving the revival.

8.  **Receive confirmation:** Once approved, your policy will be
    reinstated, and you will receive confirmation. The due bonus will be
    automatically attached to your revived policy.

Validity of Revival Approval is to be made configurable in the system as
30 Days, 45 Days, 60 Days. Policy Status to be changed to \"Revival
Approved\" upon approval of revival. Same can be reverted to lapse on
expiry of Validity of Revival.

In case the Revival/Reinstatement amount comes after the validity period
of quotation expires, the Revival/Reinstatement amount should be
re-calculated. Validity of a revival quotation is up to the last working
day of the month since the revival interest calculation formula is based
on "Monthly" frequency.

| **[Surrender of Policies:-]{.underline}**                                                              |
|                                                                                                        |
| A policy other than an Anticipated Endowment Assurance, 10 Year Rural PLI and Children policy may be   |
| surrendered for an immediate payment in cash, provided the policy is of not less than three years      |
| duration. In such a case, the insured person or the assigns of the policy, as the case may be, shall   |
| give notice of surrender, in writing, to the Postmaster/Manager of Central Processing Centre (GPO/Head |
| Office) concerned and forward the policy or a duplicate copy thereof or Indemnity bond (if policy is   |
| lost) at any Post office along with the premium receipt book, if premia had been paid in cash and loan |
| repayment receipt book, if loan principal/interest is outstanding. The concerned Post Office will send |
| all those documents to the Postmaster/ Manager of Central Processing Centre (GPO/ Head Office) from    |
| where the same will be sent to the concerned approving authority for approval.                         |
|                                                                                                        |
| For policies in respect of which premium is paid annually in advance, surrender value is to be         |
| calculated at the end of the year irrespective of the date of surrender but payment of surrender value |
| may be made when the policy holder asks for it. No bonus will be paid in respect of a policy with      |
| effect from the date of discontinuance of premia. Proportionate bonus shall be paid on paid up value   |
| after completion of 5 years i.e. if a policy remains in force at least for 5 years. ***[(Rule 55 of    |
| POLI Rules)]{.underline}***                                                                            |
|                                                                                                        |
| **[Calculation of refund to the customer (Surrender Value):-]{.underline}**                            |
|                                                                                                        |
| - The refund to the customer is known as the surrender value, which is based on the premiums paid and  |
|   the duration the policy was active.                                                                  |
|                                                                                                        |
| - Surrender facility is available after 3 years in case of WLA, EA, CWLA and YS (for PLI) and WLA, EA, |
|   CWLA and GY (for RPLI) policies. No surrender facility is available in case of AEA and Children      |
|   policy. Surrender value depends on the surrender factor, type and term of policy. Surrendering any   |
|   policy is always a loss. Bonus on the reduced sum assured is paid only if the policy is surrendered  |
|   after 5 years.***[(India Post Customer Portal)]{.underline}***                                       |
|                                                                                                        |
| - **Bonus payment**: -                                                                                 |
|                                                                                                        |
| \< 5 Yrs Policy age- Nil                                                                               |
|                                                                                                        |
| \>5 Yrs Policy age- proportionate to the reduced Sum Assured.                                          |
|                                                                                                        |
| No bonus to be paid after discontinuance of payment.                                                   |
|                                                                                                        |
| - **Formula for surrender value:-**                                                                    |
|                                                                                                        |
| **Surrender Value=** (Paid-up value+ Bonus)\*Surrender factor (Ref: Surrender factor table)-Unpaid     |
| premium-Unpaid loan principal and interest                                                             |
|                                                                                                        |
| Where:                                                                                                 |
|                                                                                                        |
| **Paid-up Value**: A reduced sum assured based on the premiums paid.                                   |
|                                                                                                        |
| **Paid-up Value** = (Sum Assured amount\* Total number of Premium Paid)/Total number of premium        |
| payable                                                                                                |
|                                                                                                        |
| - **Surrender Factor**: - A pre-determined percentage that varies based on the policy type and the     |
|   number of years premiums have been paid. The factor increases with the policy\'s duration.           |
|                                                                                                        |
| - **Proportionate Bonus**: - Any bonus that has accrued. However, if the policy is surrendered between |
|   the third and fifth year, no bonus is paid.                                                          |
|                                                                                                        |
| - **Bonus Calculation on paid-up amount**= Bonus rate\* Paid up value)/1000                            |

| **[Maturity/Survival:-]{.underline}**                                                                  |
|                                                                                                        |
| 1.  **General Rules-** All the maturity and Survival claims in respect of policies of such persons     |
|     shall be settled in Indian currency in accordance with Post Office Life Insurance Rules.           |
|                                                                                                        |
| 2.  **Maturity**- In a postal life insurance, maturity refers to the completion of the policy's term   |
|     and the date on which the policyholder receives the final payout, which includes the sum assured   |
|     plus accrued bonus, provided all premiums have been paid.                                          |
|                                                                                                        |
| 3.  **Survival Claim**-- It means a life insurance contract entered into by Government to pay a given  |
|     sum of money, in stipulated installments, to an individual or his assignee, on survival to the end |
|     of specified periods, or to pay the given sum of money in one lump sum to his legal                |
|     representatives or assigns on his death, if death occurs before the specified date of maturity.    |
|                                                                                                        |
| 4.  **Auto Paid Up**                                                                                   |
|                                                                                                        |
| \"Auto paid up\" means a policy for which 36 or more premiums have been paid and also has completed    |
| three years or more duration from its date of acceptance and the policy holder without intimation      |
| discontinues payment of further premia. However, the policy shall be maturing on the same date as the  |
| original policy, either for the original sum assured, when the future premiums payable are compounded  |
| by a single payment, or for a reduced sum assured, when the premium in respect of the original policy  |
| are discontinued before the stipulated term.                                                           |
|                                                                                                        |
| 5.  **Paid Up Policies**                                                                               |
|                                                                                                        |
| \"Paid up Policy\" means a policy which requires no further payment of premium in respect of it, but   |
| maturing at the same date as the original policy, either for the original sum assured, when the future |
| premiums payable are compounded by a single payment, or for a reduced sum assured, when the premium in |
| respect of the original policy are discontinued before the stipulated term.                            |
|                                                                                                        |
| 6.  **Maturity Benefit for Reduced Paid Up policies**                                                  |
|                                                                                                        |
| > In the event of cessation of premium before maturity age, the reduced paid up assurance will be      |
| > granted, provided premiums have been paid for not less than three years, only at the date of         |
| > maturity, that is at the end of stipulated plan term.                                                |
|                                                                                                        |
| 7.  **Survival Benefit Pay-out for Reduced Paid Up policies**                                          |
|                                                                                                        |
| In case of Survival benefit, if the policy has become reduced paid up, in that case no further         |
| periodical payments on account of survival benefit will be paid. At the end of the period, the reduced |
| sum assured will be paid along with the bonus accrued.                                                 |
|                                                                                                        |
| 8.  **Delay in Policies**--                                                                            |
|                                                                                                        |
| If the claim is not settled within 30 days from the actual date of receipt of complete claim papers,   |
| it is established that the delay in the payment of claims was attributable to administrative delays,   |
| ex-gratia payment of interest at the rate of 8% per annum on the unsettled amount of claim shall be    |
| paid for the delay beyond this period.                                                                 |

| ## Conversion                                                                                          |
|                                                                                                        |
| - This functionality provides policy owner facility to change the product during the lifetime of the   |
|   policy.                                                                                              |
|                                                                                                        |
| - "Conversion" means any alteration affecting the date of maturity of a contract of insurance and      |
|   includes not only alteration from Whole Life Insurance class to the Endowment class but also the     |
|   antedating or postdating of the maturity of an Endowment policy and consequent increase/decrease of  |
|   premium.                                                                                             |
|                                                                                                        |
| - Conversion of commutation of a policy other than CWL class to an EA class payable at the ages of 30, |
|   33, 35, 40, 45, 50, 55, 58 and 60 only will be admissible.                                           |
|                                                                                                        |
| - Conversion of policy as would put the date of maturity or the date of cessation of premium to a date |
|   preceding or within one year from the date of conversion is not admissible.                          |
|                                                                                                        |
| - Conversion will be permitted only once during the duration of each policy without charging any fee.  |
|   Second and subsequent conversions are subject to a nominal fee of Rs.20/- in each case.              |
|                                                                                                        |
| - Convertible Whole Life (CWL)                                                                         |
|                                                                                                        |
|   - CWL policy can be converted at the end of 5 years from the commencement into an endowment          |
|     assurance maturing at the age of 50 or 55 or 58 or 60 (to be chosen at the time of exercising the  |
|     option) subject to payment of appropriate increased premium. Conversion to Endowment assurance     |
|     with maturity age below 50 is not provided under CWL plan                                          |
|                                                                                                        |
|   - The option is to be exercised strictly before the expiry of 5 years from the date of issue of the  |
|     policy. However in cases where the policyholder has not been able to exercise the option may be    |
|     allowed to exercise the option later but within a period of 1 year from due date and subject       |
|     invariably to recovery of the arrears of premiums due with interest at the prescribed rate.        |
|                                                                                                        |
|   - If in CWL policy conversion not exercised policy will continue as WLA with premium ceasing at age  |
|     70, the original rate of premium remaining unaltered.                                              |
|                                                                                                        |
|   - Declaration form, consent form (from the assignee), application, original policy, bonus            |
|     certificates, premium receipt book is to be received.                                              |
|                                                                                                        |
|   - In CWL policy the bonus attached under WLA policy is altered to the amount which the policy would  |
|     have earned had it been an EA from the beginning. Bonus to be detached should be altered bonus.    |
|                                                                                                        |
|   - If loan is taken on a policy and policy is assigned to the President of India, conversion in such  |
|     cases should be allowed only if the amount of loan and interest outstanding will be covered by the |
|     amount of loan which may be admissible on the converted policy.                                    |
|                                                                                                        |
|   - Insured have to submit duly discharged policy and bonus certificates; it will then submit to PLI   |
|     Office, Kolkata for cancellation where a fresh converted policy is to be issued. This fresh        |
|     converted policy will be issued and assigned in favour of the President of India. A revised loan   |
|     bond should also be got executed from the insured. The converted policy and the loan bond should   |
|     be kept in safe custody with JAO in the PLI section of the Circle office till the loan is          |
|     liquidated.                                                                                        |

# Overview

## Brief Description

A Policy holder holding a policy other than an Anticipated Endowment
Assurance and 10 year Rural PLI policy may atany time apply for
reduction of his monthly premium and sum assured without altering of
class his policy, or after payment of premium for not less than three
years he may apply to have his policy made paid up for a reduced sum
assured free from further payment of premium the paid up value of any
policy will be the amount bearing of the total sum assured the same
proportion as the total period for which premium have been paid bears to
the maximum period for which premia where originally payable. paid up
policies will not attract bonus with effect from the date of
discontinuance of premium. Proportionate bonus shall however be paid on
paid up value after completion of 5 years i.e. if a policy remains
enforce at least for 5 years

Expectation - the above Rule regarding the amount of the paid up policy
does not apply to the following cases:-

(i) Where there are arrears of premiums, and

(ii) Where there is any outstanding loan or interest due.

<!-- -->

(2) Commutation of future premiums by payment of a lumpsum may be
    permitted at any time.

(3) In the case of policies other than Joint Life Assurance, Children
    Policy, Anticipated Endowment Assurance policies and 10 year Rural
    PLI, conversions involving alteration of policy terms other than
    reduction discontinuation or commutation of premia referred to above
    will be allowed only after payment of premia for an integral number
    of years and in any case where it is intended to extend the premium
    term or to defer the maturity date, on the production of a medical
    certificate of good health at the expense of the assured. Such
    conversions will be permitted only once on the duration of each
    policy without a fee. Second and subsequent conversions shall be
    subject to a small fee not exceeding Rs. 20/- or as may be fixed by
    the Department, in each case.

(4) No alteration from Anticipated Endowment Assurance and 10 Year Rural
    PLIpolicy to other classes of policies or vice versa, or alteration
    in the selected term or in the sum assured of the Anticipated
    Endowment Assurance policy and 10 Year Rural PLI policy will be
    allowed.

(5) No such conversion of a policy as would put up the date of maturity
    or of cessation of premium to date preceding or with one year from
    the date of conversion is admissible.

The purpose of this business specification is to outline the business
requirements necessary to support India Post's (IPO) Non Forfeiture
Options processing and commutation process & logic applicable to each
product.

# Functionality Description

Non Forfeiture Option: Non forfeiture benefits are the options which
provide that even if the owner fails to pay the policy premium and it
lapses after a specific period, the insured or policy owner gets
benefits as specified choice of NFO

India Post supports only one type of Non Forfeiture option for their
products which is Reduced Paid-Up (RPU).

Commutation: Commutation means alteration in the terms of insurance
contract including change in premium amount or sum assured. It excludes
changes in the date of maturity of the contract as specified in POLI
rules of DoP.
